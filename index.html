Covid 19 Impact on European Financial Crisis

Since the outbreak of the pandemic, the world has entered a bizarre state that we are not accustomed to. It had a massive impact on many aspects of our lives, from taking many people's lives and putting many others out of work to bankrupting many factories and causing high inflation in many countries.

In this presentation, we will try to find a logical connection between Covid-19 outburst and its financial impacts.

Number of Cases and Vaccinations

The COVID-19 pandemic has caused a significant increase in the number of cases globally. This has put a strain on healthcare systems, leading governments to prioritize vaccine distribution to areas with high case numbers. The widespread distribution of vaccines is crucial in controlling the spread of the virus and ultimately reducing the number of cases. As more people receive vaccines, the risk of infection decreases, allowing for a gradual return to normal activities and increasing labor productivity.

Inflation Rate and Labour Productivity

The decrease in labor productivity due to the pandemic has led to a decrease in economic activity, contributing to an increase in inflation rates. Inflation occurs when the supply of goods and services is disrupted, leading to a rise in prices.

Number of People at The Risk of Poverety

The decrease in purchasing power, combined with increased poverty risk, has created a vicious cycle. Unemployment and job loss have led to a decrease in people's financial stability, increasing their risk of poverty. This, in turn, reduces their purchasing power, making it even more difficult for them to afford basic necessities and escape poverty.
